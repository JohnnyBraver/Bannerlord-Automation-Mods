[size=4][b]Description[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Equipment Manager is an automated gear optimization and acquisition tool. It handles companion hand-me-downs, civilian loadout priorities, post-loot auto-equip, and optional exact armor, stealth gear, or hand-slot weapon upgrade requests through Settlement Automation Core's shared budget, cargo, and reporting pipeline.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Installation instructions[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]1. Extract the downloaded archive into your Mount & Blade II Bannerlord game directory (specifically inside the Modules folder).
2. Ensure the "EquipmentManager" folder is present under Modules.
3. Enable "Equipment Manager" in the game launcher.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Main features[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]- Automation Toggle: Disable settlement-entry and post-battle automation without removing the manual inventory button.
- Multi-Pass Headless Auto-Equip: Automatically optimizes gear for you and your companions after battle and before trading, with separate toggles for pre-trade, post-purchase, and post-loot triggers.
- Gear Cascades (Hand-Me-Downs): Unequipped upgrades are passed down to companions in party list order.
- Priority Loadouts: Choose custom priority layouts between Sneaking, Civilian, and Combat gear.
- Auto-Buy Upgrades: Requests exact armor, sneaking-item, or hand-slot weapon upgrades from merchants when your gold and reserve settings allow it.
- Hand-Slot Controls: Hand-slot buying is off by default, has its own per-visit limit, and includes category toggles for melee weapons, polearms, throwing weapons, bows, crossbows, and shields.
- Projectile Preferences: Ammo and throwing weapons can prefer stack count, damage, or both; throwing weapons ignore melee-mode stats by default.
- Spend Modes: Choose how armor, stealth gear, and hand-slot weapon upgrade requests compete with other automation needs.
- Spare Gear Protection: Keeps useful upgrades and sidegrades out of automated sale flows without changing the player's item lock state.
- Clear Market Reports: Optional concise or detailed equipment reports, with configurable item limits and sorting.[/color][/size]

[size=4][b]Save compatibility[/b][/size]
[size=3][color=#D4D4D8]Safe to add to new or existing campaigns. This mod does not write custom save data. It only performs normal game actions when automation runs, such as equipping heroes, selling spare gear, or buying requested upgrades, so those in-game results will naturally remain in your save.[/color][/size]
